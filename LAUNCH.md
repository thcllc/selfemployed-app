## 1. Hacker News (Show HN)

**Title** (≤80 chars):
```
Show HN: SelfEmployed.app – a real transition plan for displaced knowledge workers
```

**Body** (paste into the comments after submission):

```
I built SelfEmployed.app because the labor market story is no longer "be your own boss" — it's "a lot of people are going to be pushed into self-employment, ready or not."

The wedge is a Transition Diagnostic: paste your resume, get back (1) an honestly calibrated displacement risk profile, (2) three viable solo paths that leverage your actual skills, (3) a productized first offer with price + buyer + deliverables, (4) a 90-day plan, (5) your first 25 prospects, (6) today's first five actions. Not a quiz. Not a course. A real, opinionated plan in under ten minutes.

Five product decisions worth flagging:

- Horizontal from day one, not vertical-first. The bet: frontier models can produce vertical-quality output without hand-curated per-niche playbooks.
- Free Diagnostic forever; the Command Center (CRM + outreach drafts + invoices + tax set-aside) costs $29/mo and only starts billing after first paying client lands. We don't charge anxious pre-revenue users.
- AI-assisted, NOT autonomous. Every action gets user approval. No autonomous outreach, contracts, or money movement at v1. Trust earned in single approvals.
- Pipeline-first. Most new self-employed people die from no customers, not bad bookkeeping. The Pipeline Helper is the first and most-developed helper.
- Emotionally adult voice. "Self-employment is becoming the new fallback plan. Make it an actual plan."

Stack: single-file React+Tailwind via esm.sh (no build), Cloudflare Pages. Four demo personas (Sarah / CX manager, Marcus / recruiter, Priya / lifecycle marketer, Alex / junior dev side income) show the horizontal range. Try any of them.

Pre-AI integration; current Diagnostic is hand-authored per demo persona. Next ship is wiring the actual model so any resume input produces a real plan.

Honest feedback wanted — especially: which persona resonated, what felt off, what would make you actually use this.
```
